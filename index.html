Instructional Technology Observations

A summary of school-wide trends to guide our next steps in professional development.

Key Takeaways

Overall, the data indicates a strong foundation in creating positive learning environments where technology is a regular part of the school day. The most significant opportunities for growth are in diversifying assessment methods, deepening student-led learning, and shifting the use of technology from consumption to higher-order creation and collaboration.

1. Learning Environment & Digital Citizenship

Strengths

  • Norms for respectful collaboration are evident.
  • Students are generally respectful in interactions.
  • Most students use technology responsibly.
  • Positive, well-managed classroom culture is a school-wide strength.

Opportunities

  • Empower students to take more ownership in managing collaborations and digital citizenship independently.

2. Lesson Engagement & Design

Strengths

  • Learning goals are typically posted and visible to students.

Opportunities

  • Frame goals in student-centered "I can" statements.
  • Proactively design lessons with varied, scaffolded materials.
  • Help students internalize and take ownership of learning targets.

3. Assessment & Feedback

Strengths

  • Feedback is being provided to students.

Opportunities

  • Introduce a wider range of assessment strategies (e.g., performance tasks, digital portfolios).
  • Provide specific, actionable feedback for improvement.
  • Encourage student self-assessment and reflection.

4. Primary Use of Technology for Students

Dominant Use: Consumption

Activities like watching instructional videos, reading articles, or using adaptive learning software (e.g., Lexia, Dreambox).

Opportunities for Growth

Creation: Students producing artifacts like presentations, videos, or digital art.

Collaboration: Students using digital tools to work together on shared projects.

Communication: Students sharing their learning with authentic audiences.

This summary is based on data from recent classroom observations.
